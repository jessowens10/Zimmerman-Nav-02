These prototypes have been set up to explore three distinct approaches to the redesign of the Zimmermann website navigation.

Each route has been developed in response to the same brief; to create a navigation system that is clearer, faster, and more reflective of how the Zimmermann customer shops and engages with the brand today, but each arrives at a different answer about where to place the emphasis.

Please note that these prototypes are intentionally unstyled, they are not a representation of the final visual design. Their purpose is solely to test site structure, category labelling, and the grouping of items for clear user discovery and findability.

We invite you to explore each route in full before drawing comparisons.

Route 1 is structured around the Zimmermann customer's dual motivation to browse product and to engage with brand stories, presenting both with equal clarity through a focused set of four primary navigation items.

The architecture prioritises simplicity and speed, removing duplication and editorial labels from the nav in favour of a clean, product-led hierarchy. Utility functions are positioned top right, where the customer expects them on desktop.

  • 4 primary navigation items
    with no dropdown sensitivity or glitching
  • Collections surfaces editorial and seasonal content at the top level, while the footer houses secondary links
  • New Arrivals removed from the nav in favour of filtering and sorting at the PLP level, reducing structural duplication. 'New' can also be an arbitrary lens; what's new for one customer may be less so for another.
User experience gains:
  • No confusion about where things live or why they appear twice; the customer gets a single, consistent path to every category
  • Intuitive labelling with no insider language; Ready to Wear, Accessories, Collections and Kids
  • Collections sits within the navigation as an equal pillar, making editorial and seasonal content as easy to find as product
  • Utility functions (Search, Account, Wishlist, Basket) are where the customer expects them, reducing friction for returning visitors
  • Mobile-first logic applies: two taps maximum to reach any product, and a clean panel structure on smaller screens

Route 2 is structured around maximum product visibility; the Women dropdown opens a wide, multi-column panel that exposes the full catalogue in a single interaction. Ready to Wear, Bags, Accessories, and Swim & Resort are each given their own column, reflecting both the commercial weight of the accessories offer and the heritage significance of Swim & Resort as the category the brand was founded on. The result is a navigation that does the merchandising work upfront, without requiring the customer to dig.

  • Women mega-menu presents all four product pillars as equal column headers, accessible in one hover
  • Bags and Accessories elevated to standalone columns, reflecting the brand's significant investment in these categories
  • Swim & Resort given independent column status, honouring its place in the brand's history rather than sitting beneath Ready to Wear
User experience gains:
  • Extensive product catalogue visible in one interaction; no second hover or click required to discover a category
  • Column-header structure (All Ready To Wear / All Bags / All Accessories / All Swim & Resort) provides clear wayfinding and allows the customer to jump to a broad category or go straight to a sub-category
  • Collections are reinforcing brand storytelling without cluttering the product navigation
  • Cleaner label hierarchy, Women, Kids, Collections, reflects how customers naturally think about the site, rather than mirroring internal product taxonomy
  • Mobile-first logic applies: two taps maximum to reach any product, and a clean panel structure on smaller screens

Route 3 draws more of a distinction between shopping and storytelling, giving the latter a dedicated space. The primary navigation is kept lean and product-focused, while Z.Immerse, positioned in the utility nav as a live link, becomes the permanent home for all brand world content including boutiques, sustainability, behind-the-scenes footage, collections, and editorial stories.

This consolidation removes the fragmentation of brand content seen across the current site and gives the customer a clear, consistent destination for exploring the brand beyond product.

  • NEW functions as a direct link to the New Arrivals PLP
  • Accessories sits as a standalone primary nav item, ensuring high-value non-clothing categories are immediately visible
  • Z.Immerse houses editorial and cultural content, keeping the shopping navigation focused and uncluttered. Editorial content will also live through the whole website (as we want storytelling to weave along the product pages).
User experience gains:
  • Fastest route to New Arrivals of all three routes
  • Cleaner primary nav with no editorial or storytelling content mixing with product categories
  • Z.Immerse gives brand world content a permanent, premium home rather than distributing it across multiple locations
  • Accessories at top level ensures high-value non-clothing categories are immediately visible
  • Single-column Women dropdown reduces decision fatigue on hover, with Swim & Resort prominent at the top of the list
  • Collections live within a richer editorial context in Z.Immerse, rather than as a flat nav

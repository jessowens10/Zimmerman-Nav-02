ZIMMERMANN

These prototypes have been set up to explore approaches to the redesign of the Zimmermann website navigation.

Both have been developed in response to the same brief; to create a navigation system that is clearer, faster, and more reflective of how the Zimmermann customer shops and engages with the brand today, but each arrives at a different answer about where to place the emphasis.

Please note that these prototypes are not representing final visual design. Their purpose is to test site structure, category labelling, and the grouping of items for clear user discovery and findability.

Route 1: Anchored

A clean, intuitive structure built around four distinct modes: what's new, what to shop, what to explore, and the world of Zimmermann; each with a clear and separate purpose.

Route 2: Expanded

Collections and commerce sit side by side but stay clearly distinct; Ready-to-Wear and Swim & Resort are elevated as protected brand pillars, while Clothing and Accessories give the customer a direct, no-friction route to product.

Our Story

While working the markets, I had two pages of editorial in Australian Vogue. That was a significant moment because I suddenly started to receive orders from boutiques around Australia and I could feel some real momentum. Soon after I opened a small boutique in Darlinghurst.

In 1991, Nicky's sister Simone joined the growing ZIMMERMANN business. Nicky's relationship with Simone has been the cornerstone to ZIMMERMANN'S longevity and success. "I could never have achieved what we have without Simone. While the business has evolved and fashion has changed, Simone has been a voice of reason and a constant.
